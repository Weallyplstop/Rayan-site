Road signs guide

Use this handy reference to revise the most common UK warning, regulatory, and information signs you’ll meet on the practical and theory tests.

Warning signs (red triangle)

Alert you to hazards ahead so you can plan speed, position, and gear selection.

General danger

Extra caution required—look for supplementary plates describing the hazard.

Roundabout ahead

Prepare for lane positioning, give way to the right, and time your mirrors-signal-manoeuvre routine.

Wild animals

Reduce speed and scan verges—common on rural Warwickshire routes.

Steep hill

Use engine braking for manuals and adjust speed early to avoid overheating brakes.

School crossing

Expect children near the carriageway. Check mirrors and be ready to stop.

Regulatory signs (must obey)

Failure to comply is an offence—remember colour coding: red rings = prohibitions, blue circles = mandatory actions.

No entry

Do not proceed past this point. Often placed at one-way exits.

No vehicles

Road closed to all motor vehicles. Watch for alternative route signage.

Turn right ahead

Blue mandatory arrow instructs you to follow the indicated direction.

Speed limit

Red circle with numerals indicates maximum mph. Remember default 30mph in lit areas.

Bus lane / tram gate

Blue rectangles specify when only buses, cycles, or trams may use a lane. Check the times on the plate.

Information & direction signs

Help with navigation, lane choice, and facilities—colours signal the type of road.

Motorway direction

Blue background with white text. Watch for lane arrows and junction numbers near the top.

Primary route

Green panels point towards major A-roads—important for satnav-free planning.

Service / information

White background for local destinations, car parks, hospitals, and tourist attractions.

Parking panels

Blue background indicates permitted parking. Check additional plates for time limits.

Road works

Amber or black-on-yellow signs provide lane closures, temporary speed limits, and convoy instructions.
